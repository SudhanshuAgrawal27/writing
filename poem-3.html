Letters in Trees

I've been thinking again about letters carved in trees. What impulse could you conceive, That forces an imprint into nature, So plain, so crude in character? Then I think again, and how could you not? Knowing your body is soon to be but rot, Place a mark upon the world that lasts, Through rain, through snow, through lifetimes passed? Place some sign on some eternal thing, More permanent than a finger with a ring, That you were alive and here, that your life has some meaning, If only for this moment so fleeting. To have me witness your writing and wonder, Who really was here? Though I may scoff and scold, The carver is likely dead and cold, Satisfied, perhaps, that some wanderer, Chanced upon the letters of a stranger, And spared a moment of his own, To ask to the intentions of his departed soul.

— Sudhanshu
